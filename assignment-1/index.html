Photography and Perspective

This assignment compares how camera distance changes perspective in portraits, street scenes, and projection examples.

Part 1

Portrait Perspective

Close distance versus stepping back.

Photo A

Close Distance

The camera is near the subject, so facial features look more distorted.

Photo B

Stepped Back

Moving farther away gives a more natural look.

Analysis

Perspective changes because of camera distance, not because of zoom.

When the camera is closer, depth differences across the face are stronger and distortion increases.

Part 2

Street Compression

Far and zoomed versus closer to the scene.

Photo C

Far and Zoomed

Objects appear flatter and closer together.

Photo D

Closer Position

The scene keeps more natural depth and separation.

Analysis

A long shooting distance compresses the scene.

Moving closer makes the depth between objects easier to see.

Part 3

Projection Types

Perspective versus orthographic-style view.

Perspective

Perspective Projection

Parallel lines appear to meet at vanishing points.

Orthographic

Orthographic-Style View

Parallel lines stay parallel and the view looks flatter.

Analysis

Perspective projection is closer to how a normal camera sees the world.

Orthographic-style projection removes convergence and is simpler for technical views.
