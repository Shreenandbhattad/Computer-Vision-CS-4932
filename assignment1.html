Telephoto vs. natural depth

Photo C
Far + Zoomed
Telephoto compression
Photo D
Walked Forward
Natural perspective

Analysis

Photo C: Distant position + zoom flattens the scene. Objects appear stacked, depth compressed.

Photo D: Moving closer preserves true spatial relationships. Clear separation and natural depth.

Takeaway: From far away, distance differences are proportionally small (100m vs 110m = 10%). Up close, differences are large (10m vs 20m = 100%), creating stronger perspective.

Part 03

Projection Types

Perspective vs. orthographic

Perspective
Perspective Projection
Lines converge to vanishing points
Orthographic
Orthographic-like
Parallel lines stay parallel

Analysis

Perspective: How cameras/eyes see. Parallel lines converge (red annotations show vanishing points). Creates 3D depth.

Orthographic: Used in technical drawings. Parallel lines stay parallel. Approximated via extreme distance + zoom or center crop.

CV Relevance: Perspective requires camera calibration (focal length, distortion). Orthographic simplifies algorithms—used in satellite imagery, medical scans. 3D reconstruction must account for perspective distortion.
